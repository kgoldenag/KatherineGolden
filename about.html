Bio

Background & experience

OEC — Global Automotive Technology Platform
As CIO and CTO at OEConnection (OEC), a global automotive technology platform backed by General Motors, Ford, and private equity, Katherine led an organization of more than 500 technologists spanning product engineering, enterprise platforms, infrastructure, data, cybersecurity, and compliance. The technology platform she oversaw processed more than $14B in North American e-commerce and $30B in global trade annually. Katherine was a central architect of OEC’s growth story. During her tenure, the business scaled from $60M to more than $400M in revenue across three private-equity ownership transitions. She led technology diligence and integration for 10 global acquisitions, ensuring that platforms, teams, and processes kept pace with rapid expansion without sacrificing reliability or performance. Her operating model innovations were equally significant. She established Centers of Excellence in Poland and India, expanding access to world-class engineering talent while improving cost efficiency. She architected OEC’s patented API platform for dynamic pricing, embedded enterprise AI with governance and ROI-driven adoption frameworks, and drove a company-wide Agile transformation that fundamentally changed how technology was delivered.
Earlier Career
Prior to OEC, Katherine served as Vice President of Technology at Corbis, a global digital media company owned by Bill Gates, where she directed enterprise technology strategy and operations. At American Greetings Interactive, she launched pioneering digital and mobile platforms that reached millions of users worldwide. Earlier in her career, she founded a digital technology consultancy, giving her firsthand experience in the entrepreneurial discipline that would later define her approach to building and leading high-growth organizations.
